Navqor Lesson 2

Entanglement and Relational Grammar

Objective

By the end of this lesson, you will:

  • Understand how quantum entanglement affects meaning in Navqor.
  • Construct relational expressions between entities.
  • Interpret simple multi-entity phrases.

Entanglement Basics

In Navqor, two or more entities can be entangled, meaning their quantum states interact to create a shared or relational meaning.

Q₁ entangled with Q₂ → "I ↔ You" (mutual awareness or reciprocity)
Q₁ entangled with Q₂ + Q₇ → "I am connected with you"

Entanglement can modify or enhance the meaning of a superposition, making phrases relational instead of isolated.

Relational Grammar

Unlike human languages, Navqor does not rely on linear word order. Grammar is about relationships:

  • Entity A's state can influence Entity B's state.
  • Connections (Q₇) and separations (Q₈) define the type of relation.
  • Superpositions collapse based on context and entanglement.
(Q₁ + Q₅) entangled with (Q₂ + Q₆) → "I grow while the other decays" (Q₁ + Q₃) entangled with (Q₂ + Q₄ + Q₇) → "I move while the other pauses and remains connected to me"

First Multi-Entity Expressions

Interpret these entangled expressions:

1. (Q₁ + Q₇) entangled with (Q₂ + Q₇) → ?
2. (Q₁ + Q₅) entangled with (Q₂ + Q₈) → ?
3. (Q₁ + Q₃ + Q₇) entangled with (Q₂ + Q₄) → ?

Hints:

  • Q₇ = connection, Q₈ = separation
  • Think of each entity’s superposition as affecting the other

Exercise

Create your own two-entity Navqor expression using 2-3 states for each entity and describe their relationship.

Example: (Q₁ + Q₅) entangled with (Q₂ + Q₇) → "I grow while staying connected with the other"

Summary

  • Entanglement allows Navqor expressions to convey relationships between entities.
  • Relational grammar replaces linear order with interaction between quantum states.
  • Even simple entangled expressions can convey complex relational meanings.
